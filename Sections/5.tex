\section{Applications}
\subsection{Some Modally definable classes}
We may start by giving some somewhat obvious examples of modally definable classes:

\begin{proposition}{Bounded Dimension}\hfill
    
    For any given $n\in\N$, the class of polyhedra of dimension 
    $\leq n$ is modally definable
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
    Indeed it's $\Pol(S4.\text{Grz}.\text{Bd}_n)$
\end{proof}

An interesting (and somewhat surprising) example of a modally definable class is 
\begin{proposition}{Locally $\leq n$-holed $1d$}\hfill 

    The class of $1d$ polyhedra such that each connected component 
    has ``less than $n$ holes'' (we may ask $\text{rk}(H_1(X_i,\Z))\leq n$) is modally 
    definable.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
    Indeed it's $\Pol(S4.\text{Grz}.\text{Bd}_n.\chi(F_{n+1}))$
    where $F_k$ is the $k-$fork 
\end{proof}

\begin{conjecture}\hfill 

    Given the proposition above, we have reasons to think 
    that some form of bounded homology on connected components might be modally 
    definable: I suppose that inside the class of polyhedra of bounded dimension $n$
    bounding the rank of the $n^{\text{th}}$ homology group might be 
    achievable by utilizing Jankov-Fine formulas.
\end{conjecture}

\subsection{Some Not modally definable classes}
More interesting is what is \textbf{not} modally definable:

Obviously 
\begin{proposition}{Connected spaces}\hfill 

    The class of connected polyhedra is not modally definable.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
    It immediately fails to be closed under disjoint unions.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}{Contractible spaces}\hfill

    definable}
